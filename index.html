Why Beyond IQ?

... It's the equivalent of a bicycle for our minds.

Steve Jobs

While digital technology and devices have contributed to tremendous increases in productivity and creativity, developments since the mid 2000s have also had the opposite effect. Technology has been designed/trained to be exceedingly good at taking advantage of human psychology in the name of convenience. Without us noticing, we have surrendered our autonomy to algorithms nudging us to watch, purchase and do things we don't really need.


This addiction to our devices and the convenience they bring has caused us to lose our ability to think and reflect deeply. We become more vulnerable to misinformation and scams that exploit the same vulnerabilities in our psychology we are primed to accept. Society becomes more polarised as we stay in our echo chambers on social media and cease to be curious and open-minded about others views. The increasing power and utility of AI will only exacerbate this problem.


We do not reject technology, but we want to use it as a bicycle. We do not want to be driven and led by it. We have curated a set of materials you can download for free here that would kickstart your journey. If you need more structure and guidance, consider signing up for the course or joining our community. Beyond avoiding scams and becoming more open-minded, I hope this programme can help you lead a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
